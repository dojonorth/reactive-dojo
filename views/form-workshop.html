More Complex Form Logic

At this point, you should have a grasp of how bacon.js plugs together, and how it fits into the front-end.

Below, you will see a registration form. As it stands, it doesn't do much, you can fill in some information and submit it. Above the form is a list of enhancements, validations and business rules you might use bacon.js to implement. This is all entirely up to you and should be seen as an opportunity to have a bit of a play. We don't want to be too prescriptive, but at the same time, we don't want you to feel overwhelemed by choice. Feel free to look up methods to help you in your quest at the bacon.js website. We've created a file at `lib/formWorkshop.js` to build this functionality into.

Form Enhancements

  • Update full name as the user types in their first and last names
  • Disable the submit button until the mandatory fields are completed - you can start with this simply, a field at a time, and then use the .and() method to combine the two properties.
  • Ensure passwords match.
  • How about building in some password strength rules and providing a feedback mechanism for it? Maybe a meter, or if you're feeling less adventurous, some text feedback to communicate what is required (numbers, letters, symbols, length, etc).
